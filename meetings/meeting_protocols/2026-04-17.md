## 1. Motivation & Goal
* **Scientific Hook:** MCSs drive >60% of warm-season heavy hourly precipitation in continental Europe and >80% in the Mediterranean.
* **Recall from Hamburg:** Community interest in evaluating Convection-Permitting (CP) scales.
* **Primary Objective:** Evaluate models for robust MCS risk assessment in a warming climate. 
    * Demonstrate model capabilities and limitations.
    * Utilize the EURO-CORDEX ensemble runs.

---

## 2. The Analysis Pipeline (The Four Pillars)

### Pillar 1: Diagnosing RCM Physics (Evaluation Runs)
* **Dataset:** ERA5-driven (Evaluation) runs.
* **Focus:** Isolate internal RCM behavior (e.g., diurnal cycle of initiation, spatial patterns) to identify model-specific "error signatures" (COSMO vs. RCA4 vs. ICON, etc.).

### Pillar 2: Synoptic Drivers (CMIP-driven Runs)
* **Dataset:** CMIP5/6-driven Historical runs.
* **Focus:** Assess large-scale environmental representation. 
* **Method:** Classification into 6 GWT flow categories (N, S, E, W, C, A). Compare CMIP5 vs. CMIP6 driven runs. 
* **Analysis:** Use composites of thermodynamics ($\theta_e$, humidity) and dynamics (vorticity, shear, $\theta_e$ advection) to determine if models are "right for the right reasons."

### Pillar 3: Statistical Process Quantification
* **Focus:** Linking environments to storm occurrence using Logistic Regression.

### Pillar 4: Convection-Permitting (CP) (Added Value)
* **Dataset:** Representative 3 km simulations (subset as reference).
* **Focus:** Benchmark explicit physics against the 12 km ensemble. Assessment of storm morphology, internal structure (storm-centered composites), and initiation timing.
